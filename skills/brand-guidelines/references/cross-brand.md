# Cross-brand Rules — Flávia / MENSURA / MIA / PCS

## Brand separation

- MENSURA: control tower, data, risk, executive construction governance.
- MIA: quiet luxury, premium engineering performance, precision and calm.
- PCS: institutional execution, public works, restoration, compliance and robustness.
- Flávia/OpenClaw: operational intelligence layer, concise, structured, internal/executive.

## Choosing the brand

Use MENSURA when the artifact is about:
- schedule control, CAPEX, dashboards, EVM, PMO, risk, construction governance, corporate/industrial builders.

Use MIA when the artifact is about:
- high-end residential/premium projects, pre-construction, client experience, precision, patrimonial protection.

Use PCS when the artifact is about:
- public works, bids, restoration, institutional documents, government clients, execution capacity.

## Mixed-brand artifacts

If an artifact mentions more than one company:

- Use neutral executive layout.
- Separate sections visually.
- Do not blend colors into one hybrid identity.
- Use each brand's language only in its own section.

## Risk language

Allowed:
- análise, validação, apuração, evidência, risco identificado, próximo passo sugerido, condição, ressalva.

Blocked without approval:
- aprovado, autorizado, aceitamos, garantimos, assumimos, sem impacto, sob nossa responsabilidade, liberado para executar.
