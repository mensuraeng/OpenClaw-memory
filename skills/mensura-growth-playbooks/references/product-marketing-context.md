# Product Marketing Context — MENSURA/MIA/PCS

Use to create or refresh brand-specific context before campaign, copy, ad or sales work.

## Capture

- One-liner: what the company sells in one sentence.
- Category: what shelf the buyer puts it on.
- ICP: company type, size, role, buying trigger, budget authority.
- Personas: user, champion, decision maker, financial buyer, technical/institutional influencer.
- Core pain: operational, financial, reputational and emotional cost of current state.
- Differentiation: why this is not commodity engineering/obra/fiscalização.
- Proof: metrics, cases, logos, documents, project outcomes.
- Objections: price, timing, trust, “we already have someone”, internal politics.
- Customer language: exact phrases from emails, meetings, WhatsApp, calls and objections.
- Conversion action: meeting, diagnostic, proposal, site visit, document review.

## Brand fit

### MENSURA

- Category: technical-executive control tower for construction.
- Core promise: predictability, governance, risk visibility, decision support.
- Avoid: generic fiscalização, “consultoria” without operational authority, empty innovation language.

### MIA

- Category: premium engineering performance / pre-construction / quiet luxury execution.
- Core promise: tranquility through precision, technical governance and high-standard delivery.
- Avoid: mass-market construction tone, urgency tactics, cheapness framing.

### PCS

- Category: public works, institutional execution, restoration and contract robustness.
- Core promise: operational capacity, documentation, compliance and execution discipline.
- Avoid: aggressive growth hacks, informal claims, unsupported institutional statements.
